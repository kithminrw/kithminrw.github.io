I am a second year Masters Student, currently enrolled in the Master of Applied Science (MASc) in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) program at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Faculty of Applied Science of The University of British Columbia and a Research Assistant in the System-on-a-chip (SoC) Group and LSI BioSensors Group, co-supervised by Prof. Sudip Shekhar and Prof. Lukas Chrostowski. I am an electronics engineer interested in inter-disciplinary research at the intersection of Silicon Photonics, Microelectronics, Microfluidics and Biosensors and also have an interest in learning Quantum Computing and Superconducting theory.

Before starting my Masters, I was a Junior Lecturer at the Department of Electronic and Telecommunication Engineering (ENTC), Faculty of Engineering, University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka.

I received my Bachelor’s Degree from University of Moratuwa in 2020, where I completed my thesis on “Designing a Cost-Effective Dry Contact sEMG Sensor System for Controlling a Bionic Hand” with Dr. Simon Lind Kappel and Dr. Thilina Dulantha Lalitharatne.

I have completed a Research Internship in the Artificial Intelligence R&D team at Telexistence Inc., Tokyo, Japan under the supervision of Telexistence Co-founder & CTO Dr. Charith Fernando. I focused on applying computer vision and deep learning to develop a technique to detect different types of supermarket beverages using the Telexistence Robot's stereo vision camera. My work was showcased in a video which was produced by the Prime Minister’s Oﬀice of Japan, on how innovative technology will change the way people live and work in Japan.

I am interested in the areas of Bioelectronics, Bio-Sensors, Silicon Photonics and Sensors, CMOS IC Design and Optimization, Quantum Computing and Machine Learning.

Undergraduate Design Projects (Self-Initiated)

  • Self-initiated Innovation - Bionic Humanoid Arm and EMG recording device using R-Pi 3 (December 2018 - January 2019)
    Created a low-cost device and circuit using AD623 for obtaining Bio-potentials using a R-Pi protoboard for recording EMG Signals. Bionic Hand was completely 3-D printed and developed using nylon strings and servo motors mounted on the arm. This project was awarded a Distinction pass after it was presented as a Self Initiated Innovation project, which was expanded into the FYP under the supervision Dr. Simon Lind Kappel.
    [code] [presentation] [video1] [video2]

  • Automated Plant Leaf Disease Detection (September 2017 - June 2018)
    Designing a plant disease detection system aimed at helping small-scale cultivators and home gardeners. In collaboration with a plant virus indexing centre, we were able to tested our product closely with a number of selected cultivators. We focussed on utilizing multi-spectral image feeds (NIR/RGB spectra) and implementing transfer learning based training of CNNs on small datasets of domain-specific images. The product was deployed using a mobile app with edge inference and recognized as a Top Initiative at National Tech Awards. Initial versions of the product were also developed as a R-pi based system and later an FPGA based system.
